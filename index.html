Project name

The Überton Project aims to develop ready-to-use open-source audio plugins (VST) for instruments and sound effects based on concepts of theoretical physics.

In the virtual space we want to hear real sounds that are inaccessable by real instruments.

For example, n-dimensional vibrating bodies (like bell or drum), sound propagation and echoing in n-dimensional rooms or near a black holes, and vibrating strings (like a harp) so small that quantum effects enter as source of natural randomness.

The Überton project initially started with a academy course "Co(s)mical Sounds" and the first plugin was developed by J. Kipping, K. Levi, S. Lentner. The current development is supported by the Young Academy of Science and the University of Hamburg.
